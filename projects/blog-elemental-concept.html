Design process for
a tech consultancy:
Elemental Concept

At Elemental Concept, we’re a team with one common goal: using technology to drive positive change for businesses. We love nothing more than the challenge of finding the right solution for our clients, whether that’s through technology strategy, software development, cyber security, AI or data science.

In a business consultancy, having a structured design process is essential for fostering collaboration and ensuring project success.

Discovery workshops play a vital role in this process, bringing together designers, clients, and various stakeholders to transform abstract concepts into concrete, actionable business opportunities. These sessions help us develop a roadmap for creating a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that effectively balances user needs with business objectives.

During the workshops, our team focuses on generating and refining ideas into practical technological solutions while establishing a solid foundation for application design. This structured approach allows us to gain a deep understanding of client needs, enabling us to craft effective strategies and avoid wasting time and resources on unnecessary features or poorly conceived concepts. Ultimately, a well-defined design process not only enhances collaboration but also drives better outcomes for clients and users alike.

client onboarding

every new project begins with a conversation

Client meetings are held to explore their vision, goals, and expectations, uncovering the core of their business and its market landscape. These discussions go beyond understanding what the client wants, focusing on why it matters to them.

From these insights, clear objectives are set, defining success metrics, key deliverables, and a project timeline, while a communication plan is established to maintain alignment throughout the process.

the challenge of client buy-in

In a consultancy, managing client expectations and securing approval for design changes based on user feedback is essential. Design plays a key role in aligning clients with user-driven decisions, fostering collaboration and an effective process. This is achieved by involving clients in user testing sessions to provide firsthand insight into proposed changes and establishing a structured feedback loop with regular check-ins to discuss user insights and potential design updates.

research

Thorough research into the market, competitors, and trends shapes the discovery workshop agenda. Insights guide the client’s unique value proposition (UVP) assessment and tailored design recommendations, while competitor analysis and user feedback inform decisions. This foundation ensures the design aligns with user needs and the client’s vision.

tips for efficient design research

Review comparison articles to identify competitors’ design strengths and weaknesses.

Combine article insights with personal UI/UX evaluations for a comprehensive design report.

Use app store feedback to uncover user pain points and design issues.

Trust instincts; document observations with reasoning and supporting screenshots.

discovery workshop

The Product Discovery workshop is where we transform ideas into concrete visions. The workshop involves exploring the business model, generating ideas for commercialisation, and scoping out potential functionalities as well as the look and feel of the digital tool. This collaborative process is crucial for shaping the product's direction and ensuring its alignment with both user needs and business objectives.

business model canvas

The Business Model Canvas enhances discovery workshops by providing a visual framework that aligns stakeholders, identifies key business elements, encourages collaboration, and focuses discussions on user needs, ultimately guiding the development of effective design solutions.

storyboarding - persona creation & user journeys

A crucial part of the design process involves identifying key users, building their personas, and creating user journeys through storyboarding techniques, which clarifies product specifications and outlines design requirements.

concept sketching

Organising brainstorming sessions with the team and stakeholders fosters idea generation and creative solutions, while initial sketches and low-fidelity wireframes help visualise concepts and refine them based on feedback from clients and team members.

story map & product structure

An initial story map highlights the priority of product functionalities and features, which is essential for defining the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and ensuring design alignment with business goals.

user interaction flows

Comprehensive user flow documents lay out the required functionalities of the system, identifying the MVP and further prioritizing the tasks. This becomes a blueprint for the subsequent design phases.

technical architecture

In collaboration with the technical team, a comprehensive technical architecture of the system is created to understand technical constraints and possibilities, ensuring designs are both visually appealing and technically feasible. Contributing to these various aspects of the Product Discovery process allows for the integration of design considerations from the very beginning, resulting in more user-centered, feasible, and successful products.

tips for a successful workshop

Always carry a notebook and be prepared to sketch. The ability to quickly visualise ideas is invaluable. Rough sketches suffice to clarify concepts for both the team and the client, so precision is not essential.

Stay focused and limit side conversations. Product Discovery days are intense and packed with agendas, making it crucial to maintain a single discussion to ensure everyone can contribute and share their thoughts, particularly regarding design aspects.

Be flexible and ready to adapt. Each Product Discovery session is unique, and designers may need to assist in various ways, from creating quick wireframes to initiating story mapping.

design refinement & final deliverables

The design process involves iteratively improving designs based on user feedback through collaborative reviews, preparing final design specifications and style guides, and ensuring a smooth handoff of design assets to developers.

implementation, launch, & continuous improvement

scrum & ux design

The Scrum framework, integrated with a UX design process, emphasizes iterative development through sprint planning, daily standups, design iterations, stakeholder reviews, and retrospectives, fostering collaboration and continuous improvement to ensure the product aligns with user needs and business objectives.

learnings

This case study highlights the importance of collaboration in the UX design process within a tech consultancy, emphasizing how product discovery workshops, market analysis, and user feedback lead to user-centered solutions. An iterative approach, along with continuous client engagement and consideration of technical constraints, is crucial for creating final products that effectively address user needs while aligning with business goals.
