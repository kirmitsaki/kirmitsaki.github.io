As a designer in a tech consultancy, discovery workshops are a crucial part of the design process and collaboration within multidisciplinary teams. These sessions allow the team to bring together designers, clients, and other stakeholders to turn abstract ideas into clear, actionable business opportunities.

The goal is to map out a plan for developing a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that balances user needs with business goals.

During the workshops, we focus on generating ideas, refining them into practical technological solutions, and setting the foundation for the application design. For our lean team, the key outcome is a deep understanding of client needs, enabling us to craft effective strategies and avoid wasting time or resources on unnecessary features or poorly thought-out concepts.

pre-discovery

research & planning

Before the Product Discovery workshop, I conduct thorough research to ensure a productive session. This involves studying the market, analysing competitors, and identifying current trends and potential challenges. These insights significantly shape our workshop and help me prepare design-focused discussion points.

What are the key features and services offered by competitors in this market?

What trends are shaping customer preferences and expectations in this industry?

What are the primary challenges users face that are not adequately addressed by existing solutions?

How do competitors differentiate themselves, and what opportunities exist for innovation?

What market segments are currently underserved or overlooked?

How does pricing impact customer decisions compared to competitors?

What are the barriers to entry or growth in this market, and how can they be overcome?

UX competitor analysis

This pre-discovery work equips design with a comprehensive understanding of the competitive landscape from both usability and product perspectives. It allows me to enter the workshop with solid knowledge of competitor approaches, ready to contribute design insights to our own system development.

Exploring competitor apps/sites, focusing on user journeys and noting design impressions

Identifying current UX trends and brainstorming potential design improvements

Evaluating our client's unique value proposition (UVP) against existing market offerings from a design perspective

Assessing how competitors' designs address market needs

Highlighting distinctive design features of competitor apps and developing our own design recommendations

Analysing user feedback on competitor products to inform our design decisions

discovery

The Product Discovery workshop is where we transform ideas into concrete visions. The workshop involves exploring the business model, generating ideas for commercialisation, and scoping out potential functionalities as well as the look and feel of the digital tool. This collaborative process is crucial for shaping the product's direction and ensuring its alignment with both user needs and business objectives.

post-discovery

After the Product Discovery session, our team has a clearer vision of the most appropriate solution. This is where I can start bringing the concepts to life through design.

My role in this phase involves creating wireframes, followed by a high-fidelity, working prototype. This prototype is crucial as it allows our team to gather rapid user feedback before committing to the costs of full design and build.

Usability testing is an integral part of this validation process. Once the prototype is complete, I collaborate with product owners to conduct sessions with real end users, collecting valuable feedback for further design refinements.

This process not only helps validate our design decisions but also serves as a powerful tool for the team to pitch the business and secure buy-in from key stakeholders.

The workshop process involves creating several key outputs:

business model canvas

While primarily a business tool, this helps the team to understand the context in which our design solutions will operate.

storyboarding - persona creation & user journeys

A crucial part of the design process is to identify key users, build out their personas, and create user journeys using storyboarding techniques. This helps clarify product specifications and scope out the design requirements.

story map & product structure

An initial story map indicates the relative priority of product functionality and features. This is crucial for defining the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and ensuring that designs align with business goals.

user interaction flows

Comprehensive user flow documents lay out the required functionalities of the system, identifying the MVP and further prioritizing the tasks. This becomes a blueprint for the subsequent design phases.

technical architecture

In collaboration with the technical team, we also create a full technical architecture of the system. This helps understand the technical constraints and possibilities, ensuring that designs are not just visually appealing but also technically feasible and scalable.

By participating in and contributing to these various aspects of the Product Discovery process, I can ensure that design considerations are integrated from the very beginning. This approach leads to more user-centered, feasible, and successful products.

learnings

the challenge of client buy-in

Working in a consultancy it is imperative to manage client expectations and securing approval for design changes based on user feedback. UX & Ui design can help improve client alignment with user-driven decisions, fostering a more collaborative and effective design process. To achieve this: Involved the client more closely in user testing sessions, allowing them to witness firsthand the reasoning behind proposed changes. Established a more structured feedback loop with the client, implementing regular check-ins to discuss user insights and potential design iterations.
