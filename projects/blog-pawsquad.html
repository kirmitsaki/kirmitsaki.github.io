Web Design:
PawSquad Platform

PawSquad is an innovative veterinary platform combining digital and home visit services to deliver convenient, personalised, and stress-free care to our pet patients - day or night.

Go back

As the web designer for PawSquad, my role was to create a user-friendly platform that made it easy for pet owners and vets to connect. I focused on designing responsive layouts that worked seamlessly across devices, ensuring a smooth and consistent experience for all users. I worked closely with developers to optimise key features like communication tools, booking systems, and payment processes. Through user research and testing, I refined the design to meet the needs of both pet owners and vets while ensuring the platform aligned with PawSquad’s mission of providing accessible and high-quality veterinary care.

problem statement

Right now, the vet-owner relationship is centred around the vet, not the pet owner. Vets have to manage their own supplier relationships, which takes time away from patient care. At the same time, pet owners are left to juggle insurers, specialists, and wellbeing services on their own. On top of that, all the data is scattered and disconnected.

project goals

Place pet owners at the centre of the veterinary experience with a user-focused platform.

Simplify pet care by connecting owners to home visit vets and integrating additional services seamlessly.

Support vets by managing supplier relationships, enhancing efficiency, and allowing them to focus on care.

Continuously evolve the platform to deliver value in a fast-changing veterinary market.

research & testing

We conducted user research to understand the needs and behaviours of pet owners using PawSquad. By identifying five key user groups, we informed our design decisions and enhanced the user experience. User testing revealed important issues with the previous platform that we needed to address to attract more users. Our prototypes played a crucial role in gathering feedback and refining our approach. Based on these insights, we concentrated on key areas for improvement while adapting to the constraints of our small startup team.

research methods

We conducted interviews and surveys to identify the challenges pet owners face with traditional veterinary care and to find out what they want from home visit services. We also consulted with vets regularly to understand their workflows and challenges, making sure our platform truly supports them. Usability testing on early prototypes helped us ensure the platform is easy to use and meets user expectations. Plus, market analysis kept us updated on industry trends, allowing us to adapt our service to changing needs.

User Engagement Beyond Immediate Needs
Our platform's value wasn't always clear to users who didn't require immediate veterinary services. We needed to design features and content that would keep users engaged and showcase our value proposition, even when they weren't actively seeking care.

Building Trust Through Design
Despite our team's high qualifications, our design failed to effectively communicate this expertise. We had to prioritise a comprehensive redesign of our website and app to better highlight our veterinarians' credentials and create a more professional, trustworthy appearance.

Encouraging Platform Adoption
While we successfully attracted users without a regular vet and those dissatisfied with their current provider, our interface needed redesigning to offer compelling reasons for satisfied pet owners to switch to our platform.

Improving User Journey Tracking
Our design didn't effectively track the user's journey from initial discovery to conversion. We developed a more intuitive user flow and implemented better analytics to understand how users interact with our platform across different touchpoints.

Personalisation & Platform Redesign
We revamped the interface to provide a more tailored and trustworthy experience, boosting user engagement and retention. This included designing an intuitive interface for the annual pet care plan, clearly communicating its value and benefits.

Referral Program & Welcome Pack
We created a seamless, visually engaging referral system that integrates effortlessly across the app and website. Additionally, we developed a cohesive digital welcome pack to complement the physical version, ensuring users felt informed and supported.

Brand Consistency
Throughout all enhancements, we maintained visual harmony across all touchpoints, from local PR materials to online campaigns, reinforcing our brand identity.

brand & service design

As we learned more about our customers and reviewed our online marketing data, we fine-tuned our brand strategy. We wanted PawSquad to feel fresh, innovative, and more approachable than traditional vets. However, we needed to find a better balance between being friendly and trustworthy. While our local ads stayed bold and attention-grabbing, we gave our website a more polished and professional look. This change helps showcase the high level of expertise and quality service our vets provide.

our voice

Approachable
PawSquad is easy to access, like on-demand services such as Netflix or Uber. A friendly tone and quick service help pet owners feel cared for.

Reliable
Our vets are highly qualified, with top degrees and years of experience. This expertise builds trust and sets us apart from unreliable online advice.

Playful
We keep things fun to connect with pet owners who love their pets but know when to be serious, especially with veterinary consultancies.

look & feel

Our goal was to design a cohesive and user-friendly experience that builds trust and keeps users engaged. Consistent visuals strengthen our brand and create loyalty by linking our design to positive experiences. By balancing a playful and professional style, we stand out and connect with pet owners, making a lasting impression in a competitive market.

visuals

Whilst our brand strategy has always been to appear new, innovative and a lot friendlier than most other veterinary businesses, we have recalibrated the balance between 'friendly' and 'trustworthy'. Our advertising campaigns in local areas can be eye-catching and 'different', but our website presence has transformed to reflect a more restrained, formal look aimed at reflecting the high level of veterinary experience and service our vets offer.

web services for pet owners

We’ve made pet care easy and convenient for pet owners. The users can quickly find and schedule a home visit vet right from the app, or reach out for advice through video calls or text chats.

Our platform also lets them explore vet profiles to see their expertise before booking. Managing their pet’s health is simple, with access to detailed consultation reports and health records all in one place.

Plus, they can securely book appointments and make payments in-app, ensuring a trusted experience that thousands of pet owners rely on.

the vet app

In the Vet app vets can easily create, receive, and confirm home visit bookings all in one place. Updating their schedule is simple, so they stay in control of their time. The built-in GPS helps them get to their appointments efficiently. Plus, they can connect with pet owners directly through text or voice calls. Keeping client information organised is easy with the ability to create and edit customer profiles.

results & next steps

results so far

Our recent updates to the PawSquad platform have been a success. The one-click appointment feature has made scheduling much easier, leading to more home visit bookings. Users have responded positively to the in-app chat for quick pet care advice, and the "Refer a Friend" campaign has brought in many new users. We’ve also expanded our network by partnering with several new London-based vets. These changes have strengthened our brand and set us up for the next funding round, demonstrating clear growth and potential.

next steps

To make the interface even fresher and more intuitive, we’ll focus on streamlining navigation and simplifying the design for a cleaner look. We plan to enhance the onboarding experience, making it easier for new users to get started, and refine the booking flow for a smoother process. Adding subtle animations and interactive elements will also make the platform feel more engaging and modern, creating a more enjoyable experience overall.

learnings

Looking back, I would focus on building trust through design right from the start, as it became a key challenge later. I’d prioritise a more professional look for the website and app, highlight our vets' expertise more clearly, and balance our friendly brand with a sense of medical credibility. These changes would have helped build trust early on, making it easier to establish credibility and drive user adoption and retention.
